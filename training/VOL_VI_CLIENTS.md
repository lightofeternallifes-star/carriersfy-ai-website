## CHAPTER 1: CLIENT RELATIONSHIP PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 Every Client Is a Partner

At Carriersfy AI, a client relationship is a partnership. We succeed when they succeed. We fail when they fail. This is not a transactional arrangement — it is a commitment to their business outcomes.

Every client interaction — from Sophia's first conversation to the annual renewal — must reflect this commitment.

### 1.2 Communication Cadence Principles

- **Proactive over reactive.** Do not wait for clients to ask how things are going — tell them.
- **Honest over optimistic.** If something is below target, say so clearly with a plan.
- **Timely above all.** A response late by one day erodes more trust than a difficult answer delivered on time.

### 1.3 The Trust Framework

| Pillar | What It Means | How We Demonstrate It |
|---|---|---|
| Reliability | We do what we say | On-time delivery, consistent performance |
| Accuracy | Our Digital Employees are correct | Low escalation rate, knowledge precision |
| Respect | We honor their time and intelligence | Prepared communications, clear reporting |
| Proactivity | We surface insights before they ask | Monthly reports, expansion recommendations |

### 1.4 The Client Bill of Rights

Every Carriersfy AI client is guaranteed:
1. Disclosure of AI nature when sincerely asked
2. A human escalation path at all times
3. Accurate information — never misleading claims
4. Pricing transparency through the proposal process
5. Data privacy and deletion rights
6. Response within defined SLA
7. Exit without penalty if we fail to deliver
8. Professional treatment regardless of deal size
9. No public case study use without written approval
10. Regular reporting on their Digital Employee's performance

## CHAPTER 2: IDEAL CLIENT PROFILE

### 2.1 ICP Definition

The ideal Carriersfy AI client shares these characteristics:

**Business characteristics:**
- High-volume, repetitive inbound communication (calls, WhatsApp, email)
- Communication quality directly affects revenue (leads, conversions, retention)
- Currently understaffed or overstretched in communication roles
- Open to AI augmenting their team (not replacing it)
- Decision maker is engaged and accessible

**Technology readiness:**
- Uses smartphones and basic software (not necessarily tech-forward)
- Has a business WhatsApp or phone number
- Can provide business knowledge in questionnaire format
- Is comfortable with a 30-day onboarding process

**Cultural fit:**
- Values communication quality (aligns with our core principle)
- Is results-oriented (can define success metrics)
- Communicates openly (will flag issues instead of suffering silently)

### 2.2 ICP by Industry

**Tier 1 — Highest fit:**
- Dental practices
- Legal practices
- Real estate agencies
- Home services (plumbing, HVAC, electricians)
- Roadside assistance / tire services
- Beauty and salons

**Tier 2 — Strong fit:**
- Healthcare clinics
- Veterinary practices
- Restaurants and hospitality
- Insurance agencies
- Construction companies
- Automotive services

**Tier 3 — Moderate fit (may require more custom work):**
- Educational institutions
- Churches / faith organizations
- Transportation / logistics
- Immigration consultancies
- Retail (depends on volume and digital maturity)

### 2.3 Green Flags (Excellent Client Fit)

- "We're missing calls every day and losing business" (clear, quantifiable pain)
- Uses WhatsApp for business communication already
- Has multiple locations or multiple staff handling the same function
- Has tried to hire and struggled with turnover
- "My best people are spending 80% of their time on repetitive tasks"
- Has a defined budget process (not evaluating indefinitely)
- Decision maker is in the first conversation

### 2.4 Yellow Flags (Proceed with Caution)

- No clear identified pain (exploring without urgency)
- Multiple decision makers with unclear authority
- Strong existing technology (may require complex integration)
- Very small volume (<10 contacts per day) — ROI may be minimal
- Budget signals are vague or absent after Strategy Call

### 2.5 Red Flags (Do Not Engage or Disengage)

- Asking for guaranteed compliance without legal review ("guarantee you are HIPAA compliant")
- Requesting features that violate privacy principles (secretly recording without disclosure)
- Asking Sophia or any employee to deceive their customers
- History of multiple vendor disputes
- Unwilling to provide business knowledge for training
- Requesting that AI claim to be human

## CHAPTER 4: CLIENT COMMUNICATION STANDARDS

### 4.1 Client-Facing Language Rules

**For AI Employees (Sophia, Nova, etc.):**
- Never mention client names or case study details without written approval
- Never quote client-specific metrics publicly
- Never speculate about client operations beyond what is trained

**For human team:**
- All external communications should use business email (hello@carriersfy.ai or juan@carriersfy.ai)
- Communications should be written at the same level of care as any published content
- Every communication should have a clear purpose and a clear next step

### 4.2 Communication Cadence

| Event | Channel | Timing | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lead captured | Email notification | Immediate | Automated |
| Post-contact acknowledgment | Email | <4 business hours | Human team |
| Kickoff meeting | Calendar + email | Within 3 days of contract | Juan |
| Go-live notification | Email | Day of go-live | Juan |
| Monthly report | Email | First business day of month | Human team |
| 30-day review | Video call + email | Day 60 post-launch | Juan |
| QBR | Video call | Every 90 days | Juan |
| Renewal discussion | Call | 60 days before expiry | Juan |

### 4.3 Communication Templates

**Welcome message (post-contract):**
"Welcome to Carriersfy AI. We're genuinely excited to build something great for [Business Name]. Attached is your onboarding timeline and knowledge questionnaire. We kick off on [Date] — looking forward to it."

## CHAPTER 5: INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC CLIENT GUIDES

### Healthcare

**How healthcare businesses operate:**  
Patient communication is the operational backbone of every healthcare practice. Patients call to schedule, cancel, follow up, ask clinical questions, and handle billing. Volume is high, urgency is variable, and HIPAA compliance is non-negotiable.

**Common pains:**
- After-hours call overflow
- Appointment no-shows (lack of reminders)
- Staff time consumed by appointment scheduling
- Insurance and billing inquiries
- New patient intake management

**Recommended Digital Employee setup:**
- Atlas — appointment scheduling and reminders
- Titan — new patient qualification
- Orion — post-visit follow-up and satisfaction

**Industry compliance:**  
HIPAA-adjacent requirements must be confirmed by human team during Business Discovery. Sophia never confirms HIPAA compliance.

**Key vocabulary:** patient, appointment, provider, intake, referral, prior authorization, insurance, co-pay, visit, clinical

**Success metrics:** Reduction in missed calls, appointment fill rate, no-show reduction, staff time saved

**How dental businesses operate:**  
Dental practices are scheduling-intensive, heavily phone-dependent, and often face the same peak demand pattern: new patient calls, existing patient scheduling, and after-hours emergency calls.

**Common pains:**

- New patient onboarding questionnaires

## CHAPTER 8: COMPLIANCE BY REGION

### 8.1 Brazilian Clients (LGPD)

**Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD):**
- Consent must be obtained before collecting personal data
- Users have the right to access, correct, and delete their data
- Data breach notification required within 72 hours
- Personal data must be handled with documented purpose and legal basis

**Carriersfy AI response:**
- Current MVP: No customer data stored beyond email notifications (Resend)
- Lead notifications sent to team; leads do not receive system-stored data
- Data deletion requests honored within 5 business days (SOP-OPS-007)
- Sophia never stores customer data server-side in current MVP

**Brazilian business culture notes:**
- WhatsApp is the primary business channel — treat it with corresponding importance
- Relationships matter before transactions — don't rush
- "Saudade" culture — warmth and personal connection are valued

### 8.2 US Clients

**TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act):**
- Prior written consent required for outbound marketing calls and texts
- STOP/UNSUBSCRIBE must be honored immediately
- Time-of-day restrictions for calls (8am-9pm local time)

**CAN-SPAM:**
- All marketing emails must have unsubscribe option
- Physical address required in email footer
- No deceptive subject lines

**State-specific:**
- CCPA (California): Right to know, right to delete, right to opt out of data sale
- Additional states with privacy laws: Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Texas — confirm with legal

**HIPAA-adjacent (healthcare):**
- Sophia never confirms HIPAA compliance
- Human team must confirm specific requirements during Business Discovery
- PHI (Protected Health Information) is never stored in current MVP

### 8.3 Future EU Clients (GDPR)

**When EU clients are engaged:**
- Data minimization: collect only what is necessary
- Lawful basis: document the legal basis for every data processing activity
- Right to deletion: honored within 30 days (stricter than LGPD)
- Data transfer: EU data must remain in EU or transfer under adequate mechanisms
- DPA required: Data Processing Agreement with all data sub-processors

**Note:** EU market is planned, not current. Prepare compliance framework before first EU engagement.

### 8.4 Universal Compliance Principles

Regardless of region:
1. **Always disclose AI nature when sincerely asked.** This is a legal requirement in multiple jurisdictions and a core Carriersfy AI principle.
2. **Collect minimum data.** Do not collect what you don't need.
3. **Honor deletion requests.** Respond within 5 business days maximum.
4. **Never make absolute compliance claims.** Always route to human team for specific legal requirements.
5. **Keep records.** Document consent, requests, and responses.
