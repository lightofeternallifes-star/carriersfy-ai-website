## CHAPTER 1: OPERATIONS PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 The Operational Standard

Excellent operations at Carriersfy AI means:
- Every client enters the service knowing exactly what to expect
- Every Digital Employee performs at or above specification at launch
- Every problem is caught before the client sees it
- Every issue is resolved faster than the client expects
- Every client receives more value than they paid for

Operations is not a support function — it is a value creation function. The quality of operations is the quality of the product.

### 1.2 The 24/7 Principle

Digital Employees never stop. They respond at 3am, on holidays, during volume spikes. The operations infrastructure that supports them must be designed with this reality as the baseline.

Every operational decision must answer: **What happens at 2am on a Sunday when something breaks?**

### 1.3 Accountability Framework

| Area | Owner |
|---|---|
| Client onboarding | Juan + human team |
| Digital Employee configuration | Human team + AI (supervised) |
| Quality assurance | Human team |
| Client success | Human team + Orion (AI Employee) |
| Technical operations | Juan |
| Knowledge base maintenance | Human team |
| Incident response | Juan (P0/P1), human team (P2/P3) |

### 1.4 Continuous Improvement Cycle

1. Monitor performance metrics (daily)
2. Identify gaps or degradation
3. Root cause analysis
4. Fix: knowledge update, configuration change, or process adjustment
5. Re-test
6. Document and update SOPs

## CHAPTER 3: DIGITAL EMPLOYEE DEPLOYMENT

### 3.1 Voice AI Deployment

**Prerequisites:**
- Phone number provisioned (US: DID, BR: VoIP compatible)
- Voice AI provider connected
- Voice selected and approved
- Language configured
- Call routing rules defined
- Business hours configured
- Escalation path defined (transfer to human, voicemail, or call-back scheduling)

**Testing protocol:**
- 10 inbound call scenarios tested
- After-hours behavior tested
- Transfer/escalation tested
- Hold music/wait behavior tested
- Voicemail tested (if applicable)

**Go-live checklist:**
- [ ] Number ported/provisioned
- [ ] Voice live in staging
- [ ] Client test calls completed and approved
- [ ] Monitoring active
- [ ] Escalation contact updated
- [ ] Go-live confirmed

### 3.2 WhatsApp AI Deployment

**Prerequisites:**
- Meta Business account verified
- WhatsApp Business API access granted
- Business phone number verified
- Message templates approved by Meta (outbound)
- Webhook endpoint configured
- Opt-in/opt-out handling configured

**Testing protocol:**
- 10 inbound message scenarios tested
- Opt-out handling tested
- Template message delivery tested
- Media message handling tested (if applicable)
- Response time tested (<5 second target)

**Compliance check:**
- [ ] Opt-in collection process confirmed
- [ ] Opt-out handling confirmed (STOP, Para, Cancelar)
- [ ] 24-hour session window handling confirmed
- [ ] Business verification complete

### 3.3 Web Chat Deployment

**Prerequisites:**
- sophia-chat.js included in target page
- /api/chat endpoint live and responding
- ANTHROPIC_API_KEY set in environment
- WHATSAPP_PHONE_NUMBER replaced with real number

**Testing protocol:**
- Sophia modal opens correctly
- 5 conversation test scenarios
- Lead capture form submits correctly
- Strategy Call button functional
- WhatsApp deeplink working
- Fallback message displays correctly when API is down

### 3.4 Multi-Channel Coordination

When a client runs multiple channels:
- All channels must reflect identical knowledge base
- A conversation started on one channel continues consistently on another (manual context for MVP; automated in future)
- Escalation paths are channel-specific but lead to same human team
- Performance is monitored per-channel

### 4.3 Incident Classification

| Level | Definition | Response Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| P0 — Critical | AI not responding, sending wrong information, privacy breach, system down | Immediate (< 15 min) | Juan |
| P1 — High | Lead capture failing, channel down, escalation rate > 30%, knowledge error detected | < 1 hour | Juan + team |
| P2 — Medium | Slow responses (>5s), minor tone issues, FAQ gap identified | < 4 hours | Human team |
| P3 — Low | Minor copy improvements, new FAQ suggested, formatting issue | Next business day | Human team |

**P0 Response Protocol:**
1. Detect (monitoring alert or client report)
2. Assess impact (how many clients affected?)
3. Immediate mitigation (rollback, disable, or isolate)
4. Client notification (within 30 minutes of confirmed P0)
5. Root cause investigation
6. Fix and re-test
7. Re-enable
8. Post-mortem within 48 hours

**P0 Client Communication Template:**
"We've identified a technical issue affecting [service]. Our team is addressing it with urgency. We'll provide an update within [X] minutes. We apologize for the disruption and will share a full explanation once resolved."

## CHAPTER 5: CLIENT SUCCESS OPERATIONS

### 5.1 Client Health Scoring

| Signal | Weight | Green | Yellow | Red |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Chat response accuracy | 30% | >95% | 85-95% | <85% |
| Escalation rate | 20% | <10% | 10-25% | >25% |
| Lead capture rate | 20% | >15% | 8-15% | <8% |

| Open incident age | 10% | No open P1 | P1 < 48h | P1 > 48h or P0 open |

**Green:** Continue normal cadence.  
**Yellow:** Proactive outreach within 48 hours; investigate root cause.  
**Red:** Immediate escalation to Juan; client call within 24 hours.

### 5.2 Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

**Frequency:** Every 90 days post-launch  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Owner:** Juan

**QBR Agenda:**
1. Welcome (5 min)
2. Performance recap — 90 days of data (15 min)
3. Success highlights (10 min)
4. Challenges and resolutions (10 min)
5. Improvement recommendations (10 min)
6. Expansion discussion (5 min)
7. Next 90-day plan (5 min)

**QBR Metrics Package:**
- Total conversations by channel
- Intent breakdown
- Lead capture count
- Escalation rate trend
- Response accuracy
- Client satisfaction (NPS)
- Incidents resolved

### 5.3 Expansion Playbook

**When to propose expansion:**
- Client health is Green for 60+ consecutive days
- Lead capture rate is consistently above target
- Client has mentioned additional pain areas in conversation
- Client has opened a new location or service line

**Expansion options:**
- Add a second Digital Employee
- Add a new channel (add WhatsApp to a voice-only deployment)
- Add a language
- Deploy app development

**Expansion conversation opening:**
"Based on the last 90 days, [specific metric] has been consistently strong. We've also noticed that [specific gap] — adding [specific expansion] would close that loop. Would it be worth a 30-minute conversation to explore what that would look like?"

### 5.4 Renewal Playbook

**Timeline:** Begin renewal conversation 60 days before contract expiry.

**60 days before expiry:**
- Review contract terms internally
- Review client health metrics
- Prepare renewal brief (value delivered vs. investment)

**45 days before expiry:**
- Renewal conversation: "Your engagement renews in 45 days. Based on the results we've seen together, here's what I'd recommend for the next year..."

**30 days before expiry:**
- Renewal proposal delivered
- Multi-year option presented (if appropriate)

**15 days before expiry:**
- Follow-up if not yet renewed

### 5.5 Churn Prevention

**Early warning signals:**
- Response rate to check-ins declining
- Client mentions budget pressure
- Key contact leaves the business
- Health score drops to Yellow for 2+ consecutive weeks
- Client misses QBR without rescheduling

**Intervention protocol:**
1. Proactive outreach ("I noticed X — can we talk?")
2. Executive involvement (Juan direct call)
3. Value review (what are they getting that they may not be tracking?)
4. Adjustment offer (scope reduction, not cancellation)

## CHAPTER 6: INTERNAL OPERATIONS

### 6.1 Knowledge Base Maintenance

**Update schedule:**
- Minor corrections: Same business day
- FAQ additions: Weekly batch review
- Major knowledge updates: Bi-weekly review cycle
- Full knowledge audit: Quarterly

**Content approval process:**
1. Proposed change identified (client feedback, conversation analysis, or team observation)
2. Change drafted
3. Impact assessment (does this affect existing configured responses?)
4. Internal review (human team)
5. Client approval (if content affects their specific knowledge base)
6. Update deployed
7. Post-update test (minimum 5 relevant test scenarios)

**Version control:**
- All knowledge documents version-tracked
- Change log maintained per document
- Rollback possible to any previous version

### 6.2 AI Agent Updates and Retraining

**Triggers for retraining:**
- New service added to portfolio
- Existing service significantly changed
- Pricing philosophy updated
- New industry added
- Client-specific knowledge update
- Performance issue detected in quality review

**Retraining process:**
1. Document the change
2. Update system prompt or knowledge base
3. Run full QA test suite (20 scenarios minimum)
4. Soft launch (staging environment)
5. Monitor for 24 hours in staging
6. Full deployment
7. 48-hour post-update monitoring

**Rollback procedure:**
- Keep previous system prompt version on file
- If quality degrades post-update: revert within 4 hours
- Investigate root cause before re-deploying change

### 6.3 Security Operations

**Daily security checklist:**
- [ ] No unauthorized API key usage
- [ ] Cloudflare Pages functions responding normally
- [ ] OMEGA directory not publicly accessible (verify if needed)
- [ ] No unexpected deployments to production

**Incident response (security):**
- API key leaked: Rotate immediately in Cloudflare Pages env vars; audit usage logs
- Data breach (any customer data accessed): Notify affected parties within 72 hours (LGPD/GDPR requirement)
- Unauthorized deployment: Rollback, investigate, audit git history

**Access control audit:**
- Conducted quarterly
- Review: GitHub access, Cloudflare access, API key access
- Principle of least privilege: only Juan has production write access

### 6.4 Business Continuity

**Anthropic API unavailable:**
- Sophia chat falls back to fallback message (hello@carriersfy.ai + WhatsApp)
- Lead capture form remains fully functional
- Strategy Call button remains functional
- Alert: Juan notified immediately

**Cloudflare outage:**
- Full site unavailable
- Monitor Cloudflare status page
- Prepare client communication (if >30 minute outage)

**Resend email unavailable:**
- Lead capture form fails silently (fix: add retry logic in future)
- Interim: Direct prospects to hello@carriersfy.ai directly

**Domain issue:**
- Cloudflare DNS monitoring enabled
- If carriersfy.ai unreachable: investigate DNS settings immediately

### 6.7 Juan Unavailability Protocol

With a founder-led single-person human team, the risk of service disruption during Juan's unavailability (illness, travel, emergency) must be mitigated.

**Preparation (ongoing):**
- All client contacts are documented with email and phone in OMEGA/CLIENTS/
- All active deployment configurations are documented in OMEGA/PROJECTS/
- All API keys are stored in Cloudflare Pages — accessible to authorized backup contacts only
- Emergency contact: Clients are given hello@carriersfy.ai (monitored) as the fallback contact

**During planned absence (travel, vacation):**
- Activate Digital Employee monitoring (Sophia/Atlas can handle client inquiries)
- Set up email autoresponder on juan@ with hello@ as fallback
- Brief any temporary coverage on active client situations
- Schedule no go-lives or major changes during absence periods

**During unplanned absence (emergency):**
- Digital Employees continue operating (no human needed for routine)
- P0 incidents: System alerts notify by email/SMS — must have at least one trusted technical contact who can access Cloudflare Pages
- Active clients: Orion handles routine client communication; escalations wait for Juan
- SLA: Communicate delay to any client affected by P0 within 24 hours

**Recommended:** Identify and brief one technical co-pilot (family, trusted developer, or partner) with time-limited Cloudflare Pages access — for P0 emergency response only.

## CHAPTER 9: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: What happens if a client wants changes after go-live?**  
A: Minor changes (FAQs, small knowledge updates) are included in the engagement. Major changes (new employees, new channels, significant scope changes) are scoped and priced separately.

**Q: How long does onboarding typically take?**  
A: 28-35 days from contract signing to go-live for standard deployments. Enterprise or multi-channel deployments may take 45-60 days.

**Q: What if the client doesn't pass Acceptance Testing?**  
A: Issues identified in CAT are fixed — the clock does not stop. Go-live is delayed until all issues are resolved. Clients should not go live with known quality problems.

**Q: What is the minimum required to launch?**  
A: Signed contract, business knowledge approved, one Digital Employee configured, one channel tested, CAT signed.

**Q: How are API costs managed?**  
A: The Anthropic API key is Carriersfy AI's key — usage is monitored monthly. High-volume clients may require a separate billing conversation.
